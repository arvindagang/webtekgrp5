Drug Prevention week activities

The local government will spearhead the city's celebration of Drug Abuse Prevention and Control Week thru various activities starting November 23.

The event is in coordination with the City Anti-Drug Abuse Council (CADAC) with councilor Roberto Ortega as action officer. The alderman chairs the city council's committee on peace and order and public safety.

Unveiled in Monday's flag-raising ceremony, the calendar of events include a media forum on November 23, 10 am, at the city social welfare and development office conference hall and a Baguio Emergency Response to Addiction activity every Wednesday and Friday, 1 pm, of this month and December at the same venue.

This month will also see the conduct of an anti-drug advocacy campaign in the city's private and public elementary and secondary schools spearheaded by the Department of Education-Baguio and Baguio City Police Office.

A recovery walk from Dontogan to La Presa will be done on November 25 and those interested to join can visit Councilor Ortega's office at City Hall or call 446-9101 for more information.

Mayor Mauricio Domogan is urging the public to support the activities as his promised mandatory drug tests for all city government workers has been completed and results are being awaited.

Domogan's push for mandatory drug-testing of all government employees is in support of Pres. Duterte's on-going campaign against the illegal-drugs menace.

City government aims for a drug free workplace

The city government here spearheaded by the city human resource management office conducted a forum on a drug free workplace to some city hall employees last February 23 at the Baguio City multi-purpose hall.

The forum aims to protect the welfare of the employees against the evil effects of drugs; enhance the level of awareness on drug abuse prevention and control; to update knowledge of employees on the complex issues regarding drug and substance abuse; to help employees to achieve good health, avoid problems of drug abuse and increase safety; to explain how the program can benefit the employees and their family; mitigate the problems of drug abuse in the workplace, in the family and community; and serves as an opportunity for all city hall employees to implement Drug-Free Workplace program geared towards the attainment of a drug-free Philippines.

The salient provisions and penalties of Republic Act 9165 and the Civil Service rules and regulations pursuant to article V, section 47 of RA 9165 was given emphasis by information officer Joseph Frederick Calulut of the Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency.

Article V of RA 9165 mandates the promotion of a national Drug-Free Workplace Program with the participation of private and labor sectors and the Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE).

The participating employees realized the current trends and patterns of drug and substance abuse; the harmful effects of drug abuse, its treatment and the rehabilitation processes, the anti-drug strategies as well as the drug prevention and health promotion approaches in the workplace.

According to assistant city human resource management officer Edith Dawaten, the city government of Baguio has develop programs on drug abuse prevention and control through a mandatory drug testing for all city hall employees under Administrative Order No. 117, series of 2016 and its implementing rules and regulations.

It is recalled that all city hall employees underwent a mandatory drug testing last year and five employees were found out to be positive in the drug test.

However, said employees will still have to undergo a confirmatory test before the city government here can act accordingly and appropriately to the concerned employees, if ever.

Meanwhile, the city social welfare and development office continues to assist and help Persons with Substance Disorders (PSUDs) to become productive and contributing members of the community in attain a better quality of life. *Jho Arranz

DepEd to confuct anti-illegal drug program

The Baguio Division of the Department of Education is set to conduct drug intervention programs for the youth.

Based on a memorandum of agreement between DepEd ,the Baguio City Government and volunteers groups, a series of lectures will be done in various schools in the city with the objective of giving additional information regarding illegal drugs.

Baguio Schools Division Superintendent Dr. Federico Martin said aside from additional information, they hope students would not be victims of illegal drugs.

In particular, the city education division will focus on students coming from the alternative learning system who have not continued their education for them to be assisted in having a good education under the internal program on science and math.

"DepEd is planning to buy science related equipments this year funded by the City Government for the use of the students under the ALS, aside from the fast tracking of additional classrooms and school buildings as part of the K to 12 program of the government ," Martin explained.

Martin pointed out additional trainings for teachers will be done this year to ensure the quality of education and academic performances of its students.

Last year, Police Regional Office Cordillera regional director Chief Superintendent Elmo Francis Sarona started a partnership with the education sector to map out and help the schools come up with programs and probable curriculum on drug prevention.
